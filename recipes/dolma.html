Iraqi dolma

dolma is a rich, flavorful dish with a mix of stuffed vegetables. The exact ingredients vary by household, but the core is pretty consistent

  • Stuffed vegetables
  • Grape leaves
  • Bell peppers (sweet or spicy)
  • Zucchini Onion (hollowed and stuffed)

a step by step way to cook it:

  1. Prepare the vegetables
    1. Soak grape leaves in warm water (if preserved)
    2. Hollow out: eggplant, zucchini, peppers
    3. Peel onions, cut one side, and separate layers (for stuffing)
  2. Make the filling

      3. mix...

    1. Rice (washed, not cooked)
    2. Ground meat (lamb or beef)
    3. Finely chopped onion + garlic
    4. Parsley or dill
    5. Tomato paste
    6. Pomegranate molasses
    7. Oil
    8. Salt, black pepper, spices

      9. mix well!

  3. Stuff the vegetables
    1. Fill each piece about ¾ full (rice expands)
    2. Roll grape leaves tightly (not too loose, not bursting)
  4. Prepare the pot
    1. Add sliced potatoes or eggplant at the bottom
    2. Add meat pieces or bones (optional but adds flavor)
  5. Arrange everything
    1. Pack the stuffed vegetables tightly in layers
    2. Grape leaves usually go on top or bottom depending on preference

      3. note that tight packing is important so they don’t fall apart.

  6. Add the cooking liquid
    1. Water
    2. Tomato paste
    3. Pomegranate molasses
    4. Salt + spices
    5. Lemon juice (optional)

      6. liquid should almost cover everything.

  7. Cook it
    1. Put a plate on top (to hold everything in place)
    2. Bring to a boil, then lower heat
    3. Simmer for about 1.5 to 2 hours
  8. finish
    1. Let it rest ~15 minutes
    2. Flip the pot onto a tray (optional, but traditional)

The real key If the balance between sour (pomegranate molasses / lemon) and rich (meat + oil) is off, the whole dish feels wrong.
